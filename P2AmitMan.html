In Loving Memory of Amit Mann

Biography

Amit Man was the fifth daughter of David and Rachel Man, born and raised in Netivot. A vibrant young woman, Amit was a ray of sunshine, talented and full of life. She had an incredible voice and a passion for acting, starring as the lead in the play Anne of Green Gables at age 16. A top student in her high school's advanced biology track, Amit’s path was shaped by a personal tragedy—her father’s battle with cancer, which lasted three years before he passed away when she was 14. Seeing the doctors and nurses care for him inspired her to become a paramedic. Amit began volunteering with Magen David Adom (MDA) at age 15 and continued to excel, eventually completing a paramedic course, which brought her immense joy. She saw the profession as a calling, saving countless lives, and particularly loved delivering babies. Two months before her death, she became the youngest lead instructor for the paramedics course in the Negev. Amit planned to study medicine. Amit regularly volunteered at Kibbutz Bari, where she was stationed on October 7, 2023. After rockets and gunfire began, her partner urged her to leave the kibbutz, but she refused, insisting, "I need to stay because it’s dangerous." When the attack started, Amit treated five injured people at the dental clinic. Despite being surrounded by militants and running out of ammunition, she stayed in contact with her family and MDA, desperately asking for help and updating them on the patients’ conditions. In her final message, she expressed her love for her family, writing, "Please be strong if something happens to me." Amit was shot by militants and killed after making a tourniquet for herself. She died at 22, and was buried next to her father in Netivot.
