In Loving Memory of Vivian Silver

Biography

Vivian Silver, a feminist peace activist, dedicated her life to advancing gender equality and promoting peace between Israelis and Palestinians. Born in 1949 in Winnipeg, she moved to Israel in 1974, settling in Kibbutz Gezer, where she fought against gender bias in the kibbutz movement. She established the United Kibbutz Movement’s Department to Advance Gender Equality and served on a Knesset subcommittee for women’s advancement. Later, she moved to Kibbutz Be'eri near Gaza and became a key leader in peacebuilding initiatives, co-founding AJEEC, an organization fostering Arab-Jewish cooperation, and working closely with Bedouin women. Silver was also deeply involved with Women Wage Peace, advocating for an end to conflict and pushing for dialogue and reconciliation, even when her views were unpopular among her peers. Her lifelong commitment to justice, peace, and equality earned her widespread respect. Vivian Silver was tragically killed in the October 7, 2023, Hamas attack on Kibbutz Be’eri. In 2024, a new award was created in her memory to honor women advancing Arab-Jewish partnerships and promoting peace.
