In Loving Memory of Mor Shakuri

Biography

Mor Shakuri was a remarkable individual whose life was tragically cut short during the "Black Sabbath" on October 7, 2023, while serving as a commander in the Israeli police. Growing up in Sderot, she was raised in a loving family and became known for her exceptional strength and determination, eventually following in the footsteps of her family members to join law enforcement. Mor was engaged to David Fokal, a fellow police officer, and they had plans to marry in June 2024. Their relationship was a testament to their shared commitment to service and community, and they were looking forward to building a life together, even signing a contract for a new home. On the day of her death, Mor was among the first responders to an unprecedented attack in Sderot, demonstrating extraordinary bravery as she and her colleagues faced heavily armed assailants. During the intense eight-hour battle, she fought fiercely alongside her fellow officers, using her training and resourcefulness to defend the police station. She provided first aid to injured officers and sent critical updates to her superiors about the unfolding situation. Tragically, she was shot by a sniper while on the phone with her mother, conveying her love for her family and urging her mother to take care of her beloved sister, showcasing her deep bonds with her family even in her final moments. Mor's legacy is celebrated through numerous heartfelt tributes, including a significant dedication from the police, which honors her bravery and selflessness in the line of duty. She is remembered not only for her courage but also for her kindness and compassion, having touched the lives of many in her community. Her story stands as a testament to the values of service, resilience, and love that she embodied, ensuring that her spirit continues to inspire those who knew her and even those who did not. Mor Shakuri's life, though tragically brief, serves as a powerful reminder of the sacrifices made by those who serve and protect their communities.
